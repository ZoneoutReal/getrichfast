ClipStack Privacy Policy

Effective date: July 17, 2026

The short version

ClipStack collects nothing. Your clipboard history is stored only on your device, in Chrome's local extension storage. It is never uploaded, never synced to a server, and never sold — because ClipStack has no servers and no account.

What's stored, and where

  • When you copy (or cut) text on a page, ClipStack saves that text — with the site's hostname and a timestamp — to chrome.storage.local on your computer. That's your history.
  • With Pro, small copied images may also be stored locally as data URLs, the same way.
  • Your settings (pause state, ignore list, history size) are stored the same way.
  • None of this ever leaves your device. There is no cloud sync and no telemetry.

How capturing works

ClipStack listens for the browser's own copy and cut events and reads only the text you actually copied, at the moment you copy it. It does not scrape or read the content of your open pages, and it does not poll or monitor the system clipboard in the background.

Your controls

  • Ignore list: add any site (for example your bank or password manager) and copies made there are never recorded.
  • Pause: stop capturing entirely at any time while keeping the history you already have.
  • Delete & clear: remove any single clip, delete all unpinned clips, or clear your entire history in one click. Uninstalling the extension also removes everything.

Data collection

ClipStack operates no servers and includes no analytics, telemetry, or tracking of any kind. It makes no network requests as part of its normal operation.

Payments (Pro upgrade)

The optional Pro upgrade is processed by ExtensionPay and Stripe. ExtensionPay handles your email address and payment status; card details are handled by Stripe. ClipStack never sees or stores your payment information. See the ExtensionPay privacy policy and the Stripe privacy policy.
