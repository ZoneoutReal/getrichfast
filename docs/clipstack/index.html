Questions, answered.

Where is my clipboard history stored?

Only on your device, in Chrome's local extension storage. It is never uploaded, never synced to a server, and never sent to us — there are no servers and no account. Uninstalling the extension removes it.

Does ClipStack read my open pages or watch my clipboard in the background?

No. It listens for the browser's own copy/cut events and reads only the text you actually copied, at the moment you copy it. It doesn't scrape page content and it doesn't poll the system clipboard.

How do I keep sensitive things out of my history?

Add a site to the ignore list (your bank, your password manager) and copies made there are never recorded. You can also pause capturing entirely, delete any single clip, or clear everything in one click.

What's the difference between Free and Pro?

Free keeps your most recent 50 text clips — plenty for everyday use. Pro removes the cap for unlimited history, adds image clips, pinned collections, and one-click JSON export of your whole history.

Is Pro really one-time?

Yes. $9 once, all Pro features forever, every future update included. Payments are processed by Stripe via ExtensionPay; we never see your card details.
