When BlockSite is the better choice: if you need blocking synced to a phone app and across devices under one account, BlockSite has that whole ecosystem and FocusFence deliberately doesn't. If cross-device enforcement matters more to you than privacy and price, it's the fit.

What FocusFence does differently

  • The browser blocks, not us. FocusFence writes declarative rules; Chrome's engine enforces them. The extension never observes your requests — there is no browsing data to collect, mishandle, or sell.
  • A free tier you can actually live on. Seven fenced sites covers most people's real distraction list.
  • Commitment that holds. Strict mode freezes settings until the session ends — no "just this once."
  • One price. $15 once. BlockSite's monthly price passes that in five weeks.
