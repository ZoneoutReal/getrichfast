FocusFence Privacy Policy

Effective date: July 17, 2026

The short version

FocusFence collects nothing. Your block list lives on your device, and blocking is enforced by Chrome's own rule engine — FocusFence never observes your browsing and has nowhere to send it.

How it works

  • Blocking uses Chrome's declarativeNetRequest engine: FocusFence installs rules for the sites you listed, and the browser itself evaluates them. The extension does not see requests or page content.
  • The broad host permission exists solely because Chrome requires it to redirect a blocked navigation to the local fence page. FocusFence runs no code on websites.
  • Your block list, schedules and preferences are stored locally in Chrome's extension storage and never transmitted.

Data collection

FocusFence operates no servers and includes no analytics, telemetry, or tracking of any kind. It cannot see, store, or sell your browsing history — the data never exists anywhere it could reach.

Payments (Pro upgrade)

The optional Pro upgrade is processed by ExtensionPay and Stripe. ExtensionPay handles your email address and payment status; card details are handled by Stripe. FocusFence never sees or stores your payment information. See the ExtensionPay privacy policy and the Stripe privacy policy.
