MockFill vs. Fake Filler: pay once, or every month?

Last updated July 2026

Fake Filler is the best-known form filler for testing — around 120k users, and deservedly so. But its Pro plan (custom fields beyond a small allowance, sync) is a $3.99/month subscription. MockFill exists for people who think a form-filling dev tool shouldn't be a recurring bill: $15 once, and the free tier has no fill limits at all.

When Fake Filler is the better choice: if you need your custom-field setup synced across many machines through their account system, Fake Filler has that today and MockFill deliberately doesn't (we keep everything local; you can copy settings manually). If you're already happy on its free tier, stay — switching costs attention.

What MockFill does differently

  • Test data that can't escape the lab. Emails land on reserved example domains and phone numbers use the fictional 555-01xx block — a misfired test can never email or call a real person.
  • Reproducible runs. Seed mode generates identical data every time — attach the seed to a bug ticket and anyone can replay the exact fill.
  • Real test cards. The official processor test numbers (Luhn-valid, never chargeable) go straight into your checkout forms.
  • Invoke-only access. MockFill uses Chrome's activeTab permission: it cannot see any page until the moment you invoke it there.
