CopyMark vs. MarkDownload: which Markdown clipper fits you?

Last updated July 2026

MarkDownload is a well-loved free clipper built around one flow: turn the whole page into a .md file download. CopyMark is built around a different one: select → copy → paste into your notes, with the conversion quality of a hand-written Markdown document. Free covers selections and page links; Pro ($9 once) adds GFM tables, templated full-page clips, and all-tabs link lists.

When MarkDownload is the better choice: if your whole workflow is "save complete articles as .md files on disk" and you never copy fragments, its download-first flow is exactly that — and it's free. CopyMark's free tier is clipboard-first by design.

What CopyMark does differently

  • Selection-first. Most real clipping is a paragraph, a table, a code block — not the whole page. One keystroke, clean Markdown, straight to your clipboard.
  • Invoke-only access. CopyMark uses activeTab: it cannot see any page until the moment you invoke it there. No always-on content scripts.
  • Templates that match your vault. Pro front-matter with {{title}}, {{url}}, {{date}} and a one-click Obsidian preset.
  • Honest table handling. Cells with pipes get escaped so your table renders instead of breaking.
